Articles and nouns

Nouns are of course words that name objects, places, animals, people or feelings. They change their suffixes depending on gender and number.

Articles (los artículos) are words that we put before a noun to define it better. In Spanish there are definite and indefinite articles (los artículos determinados y indeterminados). They also point if the gender of the noun (masculine or feminine) and the number (singular or plural).

Articles

Definite articles

We use definite articles when we know the object or the person that we describe or when we talk about something another time. Look below to see how we use them.

Singular

  • masculine: el
  • feminine: el

Plural

  • masculine: los
  • feminine: las

Indefinite articles

We use indefinite articles when we don't know the object or the person that we describe or when we talk about something for the first time. Look below to see how we use them.

Singular

  • masculine: un
  • feminine: una

Plural

  • masculine: unos
  • feminine: unas

Nouns

Defining gender

Masculine gender of a noun is when:

  1. ends with letter -o:
    • el chico
  2. nouns coming from Greek language ending with -a:
    • el problema
    • el planeta
  3. ends with a consonant, e.g. -t or -r:
    • el challet
    • el doctor
  4. ends with suffix -aje:
    • el garaje
  5. ends with suffix -ón:
    • el corazón

Feminine gender of a noun is when:

  1. ends with letter -a:
    • la chica
  2. ends with suffix -dad or -tad:
    • la ciudad
    • la libertad
  3. ends with suffix -ción or -sión:
    • la situación
    • la televisión
  4. ends with suffix -ud:
    • la juventud

Defining number

To create a plural number of a noun we need to add a proper suffix to it.

  1. If the word ends with a vowel, we add letter -s at the end:
    • el chico - los chicos
  2. If the word ends with a consonant, we add letters -es at the end. Additionally, if before the "s" there is an accented vowel, in plural it looses the accent:
    • el profesor - los profesores
    • el autobús - los autobuses
  3. If the word ends with letter -í or -ú, we add letters -es at the end:
    • el esquí - los esquíes
  4. If the word ends with letter an accented vowel different than -í and -ú, the last letter looses the accent and we add letter -s at the end:
    • el sofá - los sofas
  5. If the word ends with letter -s, then it stays without any change:
    • el lunes - los lunes
  6. If the word ends with letter -z, the -z changes to -c and we add the suffix -es:
    • el arroz - los arroces
